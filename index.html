Project Overview & Motivation

Social media advertising is booming, making the manual detection of malicious, deceptive, and age-inappropriate ads extremely challenging.

Multimodal Evasion Shield

Traditional safety filters fail when text and images are harmless individually but harmful combined. SafeAd AI performs unified multimodal semantic reasoning to block evasive ads.

Age-Aware Restrictions

Integrates advanced age-confidence indicators and user age-verification models to prevent adult-targeted advertising (alcohol, vaping, gambling) from slipping through to minors.

Explainable AI (XAI)

Instead of opaque binary decisions, the framework provides transparent risk metrics and detailed policy violation explanations using advanced language generation rules (inspired by HatReD).

Problem Statement

"Development of a multi-modal AI-based trust and safety framework for social media platforms that detects harmful advertisements and performs age-aware content moderation by integrating visual, textual, and contextual information to prevent inappropriate content from reaching users."

Key Objectives

  • Multimodal Pipeline: Fuse graphic OCR, visual features, and NLP contexts into a central assessment matrix.
  • Proactive Filtering: Intercept fraud, explicit themes, and scams pre-publication.
  • Age Confidence Analysis: Prevent age misrepresentation issues from exposing minors to adult marketing.
  • Human-in-the-Loop (HITL): Route borderline violations to manual queues to optimize reviewer workloads.

Framework Architecture

How SafeAd AI digests, analyzes, and decides on social media advertisements in real-time.

1

Input Ingestion & Metadata Module I

The advertiser submits the ad creative, including the graphic file (image/video), text copy, target audience demographics (configured age range, location), and advertising category metadata.

2

Multimodal Feature Extraction Module II

SafeAd AI activates parallel extractors:
• Optical Character Recognition (OCR): Pulls embedded graphic text out of the image.
• Computer Vision (CV): Detects restricted items, logos, and visual metaphors.
• NLP Encoder: Analyzes ad copy semantics, sentiment, and intent.

3

Age-Confidence Engine Module III

Validates targeting compliance. It checks if the ad promotes age-restricted services/products (alcohol, financial products, vaping) and computes an age-appropriateness confidence score based on the extracted visual/textual semantic features.

4

Explainable AI (XAI) Decider Module IV

Integrates the extracted features into a decision matrix. Utilizing reasoning heuristics derived from the HatReD paradigm, it outputs an overall Risk Score (0-100%) and generates a detailed, natural language explanation detailing which platform policies are violated.

5

Human-in-the-Loop (HITL) Queue Module V

If the risk score lies in the borderline range (e.g. 40-70% uncertainty), the ad is temporarily held and routed to a prioritized human moderation queue, complete with the XAI-generated policy flags to accelerate manual review.

Evaluation & Reference Datasets

SafeAd AI is grounded on the state-of-the-art multimodal dataset benchmarks mentioned in our base paper, helping the model learn implicit semantic violations.

Classification
10,000+
Images

Facebook Hateful Memes (FHM)

Created by Meta AI, this dataset uses custom-built memes with visual and textual confounders, making isolated text or image analysis insufficient.

Key Challenge Addressed

Multimodal confounding. For example, an image of a skunk is harmless; the text "something smells bad here" is harmless. Combined, they target a group of people, making it hateful. SafeAd AI uses FHM-style reasoning to prevent ads from hiding scams or hate in visual metaphors.

Example Representation

"Look at all these immigrants coming over"

(Image of a desert. Multimodal fusion detects xenophobic context by combining sand/crossing visual cues with text.)

Multi-Label
10,000
Memes

MAMI Dataset (SemEval 2022)

Multimedia Automatic Misogyny Identification dataset dedicated to detecting misogynous content, shaming, stereotypes, objectification, and violence.

Key Challenge Addressed

Multi-label classification of gender-based harassment. Useful for enforcing platform advertising rules regarding hate speech, graphic violence, and harassment targeting specific genders.

Example Representation

"Get back in the kitchen where you belong"

(Targeted stereotype violation. Explicitly detected and categorized under misogyny/stereotype labels.)

Harm Intensity
3,544
Twitter Memes

HarMeme (COVID-19 Harm)

Real-world dataset collected from Twitter analyzing Covid-19 misinformation, classification of harm intensity (harmless, partially, very harmful) and targeted groups.

Key Challenge Addressed

Misinformation and harm severity detection. SafeAd AI utilizes harm-intensity metrics to separate benign political criticism from harmful health scams, vaccine misinformation, and societal scaremongering.

Example Representation

"The microchips are ready, get your vaccine!"

(COVID-19 related conspiracy. Labeled as partially/very harmful due to health misinformation.)

Generative XAI
3,000+
Explanations

HatReD (Hateful memes with Reasons)

Hateful memes with Reasons Dataset, annotating implicit hateful metaphors with the underlying contextual socio-cultural reasons.

Key Challenge Addressed

Interpretability in moderation. Rather than just returning "Hate" or "Not Hate", the dataset trains models to write explanations of the metaphors. This directly powers the Explainable AI (XAI) engine in SafeAd AI, giving human reviewers transparent justifications.

Example Representation

"Haven't heard you're not halal?"
XAI Reason: "The meme combines a cartoon Muslim figure carrying a bomb with a threatening text. This metaphorically associates Muslims with terrorism, violating religious safety guidelines."
